Horned Helmets

The iconic horned helmet that we know from popular culture was actually a fantastical creation dreamt up by costume designer Carl Emil Doepler for an 1876 production of Wagner's Der Ring des Nibelungen.

Poor Hygeine

Vikings were a lot more hyggenic than we think. Archeoligist have found tools such as combs, washbowls, rinses etc. The old Norse word for Saturday, Laugardagr, translates into "Washing Day".

Viking Giants

In 921 an Arab Traveller, Ahmad Ibn Fadlan, came into contact with the Rus vikings. He was impressed by their physicality and described them as tall as datepalms. The average height was in fact a bit shorter than today, 172cm tall.

Weapon of choice

Battle axes were one of the most common weapons used by vikings warriors, but even more common was the spear. It was cheaper to craft as it required less steel than the axe and sword. Swords were only for the wealthy vikings.
